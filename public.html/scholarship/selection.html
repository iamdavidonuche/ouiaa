Selection Criteria

Each application will be scored out of 100 points across the following categories:

  • Academic Performance (20 points): Based on CGPA and academic transcript. Higher scores reflect stronger performance and consistency.
  • Personal & Financial Impact Statement (20 points): Judged on the depth, authenticity, and persuasiveness of the applicant’s narrative, as well as clarity and structure.
  • Leadership & Community Engagement (20 points): Awarded for meaningful involvement in extracurricular or community-based activities, especially where initiative and responsibility are evident.
  • Presentation and Communication (10 points): Includes quality of writing, completeness of the form, and how well the candidate communicates their story and ambitions.
  • Interview (30 points): This will assess the applicant's self-awareness, clarity of purpose, and ability to articulate the impact of the scholarship.
Applications that score highly across all areas and align with the values of the programme will be prioritised.
